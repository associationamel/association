What We Do

1

reading & righting

Teaching kids to read and write is a fundamental aspect of early childhood education.

2

Painting

Conduct art and sensory exploration classes tailored to the sensory needs of children with autism. These classes can offer a creative outlet for self-expression while accommodating sensory sensitivities. Providing various textures, colors, and tools in a controlled setting allows for a positive and enriching experience.

3

Games

Organize structured playgroups that provide a supportive environment for children with autism to engage in play and social interactions. These playgroups can incorporate activities designed to promote communication, cooperation, and sensory exploration. Trained facilitators can guide the interactions and ensure a positive and inclusive experience.

4

Music

Implement music and movement therapy sessions designed for children with autism. Music therapy can have therapeutic benefits, promoting emotional expression, communication, and motor skills. Structured movement activities, such as dance or yoga, can also contribute to physical well-being and sensory integration.
